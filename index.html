About the Organization

Science Education Inspired by Real Research

Wildflower Data and Learning was founded to bring the tools and habits of scientific inquiry beyond the walls of universities and research institutions.

Our programs help learners of all ages move beyond memorizing facts and toward actively investigating questions, collecting evidence, analyzing patterns, and communicating discoveries.

Whether working with elementary students, educators, families, or adult learners, we believe meaningful STEM education happens when people engage directly with the world around them.

By combining hands-on exploration, data literacy, environmental observation, and creative problem solving, Wildflower helps learners develop scientific skills alongside a deeper understanding of their communities and ecosystems.

Meet the Founder

Dr. Reshma Nargund

Dr. Reshma Nargund is an environmental health scientist, educator, and founder of Wildflower Data and Learning.

She earned her PhD in Environmental Epigenetics and Molecular Genetics from Duke University, where her research examined how environmental exposures influence human health across early development.

Her work integrated environmental science, epidemiology, computational biology, and large-scale data analysis.

Throughout her career, she has worked across research, policy, education, and community engagement. She has taught learners ranging from elementary school students to university students and believes scientific inquiry is not reserved for professional scientists; it is a way of thinking that can enrich decision-making, creativity, and civic engagement throughout life.

Wildflower Data and Learning grew from a simple idea: everyone should have opportunities to investigate real questions, work with evidence, and experience the excitement of discovery.

Paper Marbling: Art, History, Science

A 3-week evening course exploring paper marbling as both a material practice and a lens for thinking across art, science, and cultural history — from its transnational origins to the physics of surface tension and fluid motion, with connections to phenomena like oil spill dynamics. Our Approach

From Observation to Stewardship

Wildflower's programs help learners move from curiosity to informed action by teaching the practices of scientific inquiry. Participants learn how to ask meaningful questions, collect evidence, identify patterns, communicate findings, and become thoughtful stewards of their communities and environments.
